## 5) Goal-Wise Roadmap (Based on Strategy B)

### **Goal 1: iPhone (₹90,000 in 12 months)**
*   **Monthly Contribution:** ₹7,500
*   **Instrument:** Low Duration Debt Fund (Capital Preservation)
*   **Feasibility (100%):** This is a mathematical certainty as the instrument is designed to protect capital. The value will not be lower than your contributions.
*   **Sensitivity Analysis:** If returns are 2% lower than expected (e.g., 4% instead of 6%), the shortfall would be minimal (~₹1,100). You can easily cover this with one month's residual surplus.

### **Goal 2: Bike (₹200,000 in 24 months)**
*   **Monthly Contribution:** ₹8,333
*   **Instrument:** Balanced Advantage Fund (Dynamic Risk Management)
*   **Feasibility (90%):** Highly probable. A BAF is designed to reduce equity exposure as markets rise, locking in gains, and increase it when markets fall, buying cheap. This mechanism protects the corpus for a 2-year horizon.
*   **Sensitivity Analysis:** If average returns are 2% lower than expected (e.g., 8% instead of 10%), the corpus would be ~₹4,800 short after 24 months. This can be covered by a minor one-time allocation from the surplus or delaying the purchase by one month.
*   **Alternative Safe Plan:** If you are risk-averse, contribute ₹8,700/month to a Debt Fund yielding 7%. This guarantees the amount with no market risk.

## 6) Debt & Cashflow Plan

*   **Debt Details:** Principal: ₹10,000, Interest Rate: 5% p.a., Tenure: 12 months, EMI: ~₹856.
*   **Option 1: Continue EMIs (Recommended by Majority):**
    *   **Rationale:** The interest cost is low (~₹275 total). The ₹10,000 lump sum, if invested even conservatively, can potentially earn a higher return than 5%. This is the mathematically optimal choice.
    *   **Impact:** Your monthly investable surplus is ₹33,144.
*   **Option 2: Lump-Sum Prepayment (Recommended by Debt/Execution Agents):**
    *   **Rationale:** Simplifies cash flow and finances. Provides a psychological win and a guaranteed 5% "return" by avoiding future interest. Frees up ₹856/month from Month 2 onwards.
    *   **Action:** Use ₹10,000 from Month 1's surplus to close the loan.
    *   **Impact:** Month 1 surplus is reduced to ₹24,000. From Month 2 onwards, your investable surplus becomes a clean ₹34,000.
*   **Final Recommendation:** **Option 2 (Lump-Sum Prepayment).** The psychological benefit and simplicity outweigh the minimal financial upside of Option 1 for such a small debt amount.

## 7) Risk Controls & Contingency Triggers

*   **Emergency Fund:** The primary risk control. A ₹48,000 fund covers 3 months of essential expenses. **Trigger:** Do not dip into this for goals. Replenish immediately if used.
*   **Asset Allocation Rebalancing:**
    *   **Trigger 1:** Calendar-based. Review and rebalance the long-term portfolio back to its 60/20/20 (Equity/Debt/Gold) target every 6 months.
    *   **Trigger 2:** Threshold-based. If any asset class deviates by ±15% from its target allocation (e.g., Equity grows to 69% or falls to 51%), rebalance.
*   **Goal Corpus Protection:**
    *   **Trigger:** If the value of the **Bike fund (BAF)** falls below 90% of the contributed capital amount 6 months before the goal date, switch the future SIPs to a Debt Fund to preserve capital.
*   **Crypto Risk Control (If using Strategy C):**
    *   **Rule:** Allocation never exceeds 5% of the total portfolio value.
    *   **Profit-Taking Trigger:** If the value of the crypto allocation doubles (100% gain), sell 50% of the holding and reinvest the proceeds into the core Debt or Gold allocation.
*   **Personal Risk:** **Term Life Insurance:** Consider acquiring a term life insurance cover as part of comprehensive financial protection. **Health Insurance:** Ensure you have adequate corporate or personal health insurance.
